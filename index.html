About Me

I am an incoming Computer Science Ph.D. student at Duke University. My research interests span across various aspects of Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. In particular, I am interested in extending the capabilities of NLP systems across language barriers and developing robust, interpretable, and equitable human language technologies for social good.

Currently, I am working with Professors Antonios Anastasopoulos and Yulia Tsvetkov on dialect classification and model interpretability.

Previously, I completed my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at George Mason University, where I worked closely with Professor Antonios Anastasopoulos. During my undergraduate studies, I also spent some time working in industry. In 2022, I interned at IQVIA as a machine learning engineer, where I worked with Dr. Chang Liu on recommendation systems and interpretability. Prior to that, I interned at AWS, working with Dr. Aziz Nazha on image classification for CT scans. I also spent about a year interning as a software engineer at Precisely (previously Winshuttle), where I worked on full-stack web development.
